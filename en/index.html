The Matilda Effect is the recurring and systematic denial or minimization of women scientists’ contributions, whose work is often credited to male colleagues.
This phenomenon was first described by Matilda Joslyn Gage.

Discover Jocelyn Bell Burnell and the Matilda Effect — making visible the forgotten women of science.
In 1967, Jocelyn Bell Burnell detected pulsars, the silent heartbeat of the universe.
Literature restores recognition to Marie Curie, Franklin, and many other women of science.
